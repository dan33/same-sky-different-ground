Prints & charts

The book's diagrams, made for the wall

Every chart in Same Sky, Different Ground was drawn by hand for this coast — built from real elevation and geology, not clip art. Now the best twelve are print-ready files you can hang: in a classroom, an office, a field station, a shed wall, a hallway between the door and the boots.

They aren't decoration pretending to be science. Each one teaches something true about how this coast is put together — why rainforest sits on the range and wildflowers on bleached sand, how a dune holds a lake, where fire draws its line. Buy the file, print it at whatever size suits your wall, and read it every time you walk past.

Why buy the file when the website is free?

  • Resolution. The web images are small and soft — fine on a phone, mush on a wall. These files are built at true print resolution, so the fine labels and hairlines stay sharp at A3.
  • Print size & licence. A screenshot is licensed for looking at, not for printing and displaying. Buying the file gives you a clean, high-resolution copy licensed for personal and classroom use — hang it, teach from it, print a class set.
  • It's the honest version. These are the author's own diagrams, drawn from real elevation and geology — not stock art. The file is the thing as it was meant to be seen.
  • It keeps the lights on. The website and its companion book are a one-person project, priced to cover the cost of making them and no more. A few dollars for a chart is what keeps the free site free.
